Mid-Term Project

This semester, I explored food delivery data to find patterns in customer behavior, order timing, and efficiency. I used these insights to better understand how deliveries can be improved and made more reliable.

  • Learn more

    • Project Overview

    My Project:

    Tracks food delivery orders from 2024

    Includes data such as order dates, restaurants, and delivery times

    Helps identify trends in customer behavior

    Analyzes delivery efficiency and performance

    This dataset provides a strong foundation for examining how demand changes over time and how delivery systems respond.

    Key Questions

    This project focuses on three main questions:

    1-Is there a relationship between delivery time and customer ratings?

    2-Which holiday has the highest delivery time?

    3-How do delivery times vary depending on the type of food or size of the order?

    These questions guide the analysis and help uncover meaningful patterns in the data.

    Key Findings

    Holiday Impact: Certain holidays lead to longer delivery times September 2nd had the highest total delivery minutes.

    Food Type & Order Size: Delivery time does not significantly change based on food type. Larger orders do not necessarily take longer to deliver.

    Customer Ratings: Delivery speed has little to no impact on customer ratings. Late deliveries do not strongly affect how customers rate their experience.

    Conclusion

    Holidays increase demand and put pressure on delivery systems.

    Delivery time is not heavily influenced by order size or food type.

    Customer ratings are not strongly affected by delivery speed.

    Overall, the data shows that external factors like timing (holidays) matter more than order details when it comes to delivery performance.
